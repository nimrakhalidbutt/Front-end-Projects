Our Craft

Built by hand,
tested by decades.

Our movements are assembled by a bench of eleven watchmakers, each responsible for a single caliber from first plate to final regulation. A watch leaves the workshop only after fourteen days on the timing machine.

Cases are milled from 316L steel or 18k gold, then hand-polished with a rotating series of diamond pastes — a finish no machine has yet matched.

Heritage

Nearly forty years
at the bench.

A short history of a workshop that never chased scale — only precision.

1987

The workshop opens

Founders Eleanor Wren and Thomas Ashby open a two-room atelier in the Jura mountains, restoring antique movements for local collectors.

2003

The first in-house caliber

After sixteen years of restoration work, the workshop completes Caliber WA-1 — its first movement built entirely from scratch.

2015

The Kepler chronograph

A column-wheel chronograph is added to the collection, still assembled by a single watchmaker start to finish.

2026

Five pieces, one standard

The current collection is finalized — five watches, each produced in runs of under 200 a year.
